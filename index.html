Gender Inequality Index

The Gender Inequality Index (GII) is an index developed by the United Nations to measure gender inequality. 1 is most unequal and 0 is most equal.

World Map of Gender Inequality - 2021

Here you can see a geographic representation of how the GII varies across the world as of 2021. Countries with no data are in grey.


GDP per Capita's Effect on Gender Inequality - 2021

The above chart shows the correlation between GDP per capita and gender inequality. It can be seen that richer countries tend to have more gender equality. Some countries have been ommitted due to lack of accurate GDP per capita data for 2021.


Index Compared with Other Measures of Gender Inequality - 2021

In addition to the index, the UN also measures specific aspects of gender inequality. The above scatterplots show the percent of the female population ages 25 and older with at least some secondary education, female labour force participation rate for ages 15 and older, and percentage of seats in parliament held by women.


Change in Index over Time

The above chart shows change in the GII over time by region. The regions are determined by the UN and are different from continents. It can be seen that, overall, each region has become more equal over time.
